## 2. WHAT PROBLEM ARE YOU SOLVING?

Product teams spend 40+ hours and $5K–$200K per evaluation cycle synthesizing fragmented user feedback from Reddit, app stores, and support tickets into actionable product strategy — because no existing tool forces structured adversarial challenge against its own conclusions, and single-model AI analysis produces confident monologues, not stress-tested findings.

The root cause is architectural. When you ask ChatGPT or Claude to evaluate a product, one model generates one perspective with zero structured disagreement, zero evidence grounding in real user data, and zero mechanism to challenge its own assumptions. The output sounds authoritative but is fundamentally untested. McKinsey solves this with human teams who challenge each other's conclusions — but that costs $200K+ and takes 6–12 weeks. UserTesting provides real user voices at $49/response with 2–5 day turnaround, but the synthesis is still manual. The War Room compresses McKinsey's adversarial rigor and UserTesting's real-user evidence into a 10-minute automated pipeline backed by 31,668 curated review chunks across 20 products.

We proved this problem is real because every team we showed the output to immediately understood it. When Clerion's founder saw "Lack of tooltips for metrics like 'Exposure' and 'Scenario Impact'" as a MAJOR friction point with a QUICK_WIN fix effort tag — that's not a summary, that's a sprint ticket. When HealthAI's founder saw 10 side-by-side comparison cards pairing their frames against ClickUp's onboarding flow with similarity scores ranging from 0.53 to 0.74 — they didn't need convincing. They started fixing their welcome screen.

## 3. WHAT EXISTING SOLUTION ARE YOU REPLACING, AND WHY WOULD SOMEONE SWITCH?

The existing landscape splits into four buckets, and every one of them fails in a specific way. **UserTesting** charges $49 per individual response with 2–5 day turnaround using human panels — real feedback, but painfully slow and expensive at scale. **McKinsey/BCG** charges $200K+ for a competitive product analysis over 6–12 weeks — thorough but inaccessible to anyone without enterprise budgets. **ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity** is instant and cheap, but single-model, no evidence grounding, no adversarial structure — you're getting one AI's opinion that agrees with itself. **Kraftful, Dovetail, and Aurelius** are AI-native qualitative research tools, but they all use single-model synthesis without adversarial challenge — they summarize, they don't stress-test.

The War Room is not 10% better — it's structurally different. Time compression: 40 hours of manual synthesis → 10 minutes (240x). Cost: $200K consulting engagement → $0 when running locally on DGX Spark, or pennies per call on cloud API (our HealthAI analysis ran three parallel gpt-4o specialists in 11.3 seconds). Evidence density: our ChromaDB corpus contains 31,668 real user review chunks across 20 products sourced from Reddit, Hacker News, and Google Play — versus zero evidence grounding in ChatGPT. Adversarial rigor: three competing model architectures (Llama 3.3 70B, Qwen3 32B, Mistral Small 24B) with mandatory AGREE/DISAGREE labels and a stubbornness rule preventing premature concession — versus one model agreeing with itself. MIT research (Du et al., 2023) validates this: multi-model debate achieves 91% factual accuracy versus 82% for same-model debate. Mitsubishi Electric independently validated the same adversarial pattern for manufacturing QA in January 2026.

The switching trigger is simple: a PM who sees our Notion verdict (64/100, YES WITH CONDITIONS, "73% of churn mentions cite findability" as the top fix with ~30% estimated retention impact) realizes they just got in 4 minutes what would take their team weeks to produce manually — and it came with citations they can verify.

## 4. WHAT ARE YOUR TWO MAIN FEATURES?

### Feature 1: Adversarial Multi-Model Debate

This is not three copies of GPT having a polite conversation. The War Room routes three genuinely different open-weight model architectures — Llama 3.3 70B (First-Timer), Qwen3 32B (Daily Driver), and Mistral Small 24B (Buyer) — through a structured 4-round sequential debate with full context chaining (R1→R2, R1+R2→R3, R1+R2+R3→R4). Different training data produces different biases produces real disagreement. Each round requires mandatory AGREE/DISAGREE labels with cited evidence, and a stubbornness rule prevents premature concession — agents cannot concede a point unless the challenger provides specific cited evidence. This directly prevents the "agreement collapse" failure mode documented in Du et al., where multi-agent systems converge to consensus within 2 rounds.

The debate is grounded in 31,668 real user review chunks from Reddit, Hacker News, and Google Play across 20 products, retrieved by a 20-agent parallel reconnaissance swarm that queries ChromaDB across product dimensions in 1–3 seconds. The output is a structured verdict: score (1–100), BUY/PASS/CONDITIONS decision, and sprint-ready findings with severity ratings and evidence citations. This works end-to-end — our TaskFlow DGX run (`demo_outputs/taskflow_dgx_run.json`) scored 65/100, NEEDS_WORK, with three specialists (Strategist, UX Analyst, Market Researcher) each producing structured JSON sections. The Strategist identified "MVP stage with only 12 beta users lacks validation at scale" as a key risk. The UX Analyst found "Sidebar lacks visual hierarchy for 8 ungrouped navigation items" at severity 7/10 with a 1–2 day quick-win fix. The Market Researcher identified Trello (HIGH threat) and ClickUp (MEDIUM threat) as competitive pressures with specific evidence.

### Feature 2: Video Walkthrough QA Pipeline

Upload a screen recording of your product and the system runs a 6-stage automated pipeline: (1) ffmpeg extracts key frames from the video, (2) GPT-4o Vision analyzes each frame for UX patterns, (3) frames are matched against a 69-app competitor screenshot suite via sentence-transformer embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), (4) evidence is curated from ChromaDB with per-theme sentiment analysis, (5) three AI specialists (Strategist, UX Analyst, Market Researcher) produce structured analysis sections, (6) a partner review cross-validates all three analyses and assembles the final deliverable with a scored verdict.

We ran this end-to-end on HealthAI today — a real hackathon team's real-time health tracking app. The system extracted 10 frames, analyzed each with GPT-4o Vision, generated 10 side-by-side comparison cards pairing HealthAI's screens against ClickUp's UI (similarity scores from 0.53 to 0.74), then ran three specialist rounds in 11.3 seconds of parallel cloud inference. The deliverable at `sessions/bf39ba5a` includes: Strategist score 3.0/10 identifying "AI-powered health insights are not clearly differentiated" as CRITICAL risk, UX Analyst score 4.5/10 with friction map showing "Welcome Screen lacks interactive cues" as MAJOR severity with QUICK_WIN fix effort, and a final verdict of 5.8/10 NEEDS_WORK with Monday action item: "Add a progress indicator and interactive tutorial to the welcome screen by Friday." The HealthAI team started implementing these findings immediately.

## 5. WHAT IS YOUR INNOVATIVE USE OF AI?

AI is not a feature bolted onto an existing workflow. AI IS the product — every component is AI-native, and removing AI leaves you with nothing. The core innovation is a three-layer AI orchestration protocol that goes far beyond "call an LLM and display the response."

**Layer 1 — Meta-Agent Persona Generation:** Before any analysis begins, a meta-agent dynamically generates product-specific adversarial personas. This is not templates — analyzing Notion produces fundamentally different archetypes than analyzing a health tracker. The persona generator uses an 8-category software taxonomy to create role-appropriate agents with calibrated backstories, evidence preferences, and analytical frameworks. The First-Timer trusts App Store reviews and Reddit first impressions. The Daily Driver trusts long-form technical discussions and Hacker News threads. The Buyer trusts pricing comparisons and business reviews. These aren't arbitrary — they're designed to maximize epistemic divergence.

**Layer 2 — Reconnaissance Swarm:** Twenty parallel scout agents sweep our 31,668-chunk ChromaDB corpus across 20 product dimensions (onboarding, pricing, performance, integrations, collaboration, mobile stability, etc.) in 1–3 seconds. This is not a single RAG query — it's a coordinated intelligence operation that pre-gathers evidence before debate begins, using sub-50ms cosine similarity retrieval on 384-dimensional sentence-transformer embeddings. The swarm result is injected as an intelligence briefing into the first debate round, giving agents a comprehensive evidence foundation to cite from.

**Layer 3 — Adversarial Debate with Thermal-Adaptive Execution:** Three distinct open-weight architectures (Llama 3.3 70B / Qwen3 32B / Mistral Small 24B) conduct structured debate with mandatory disagreement rules. But here's what makes this genuinely novel: after 7 DGX Spark thermal crashes during sustained multi-model inference, we built a hardware-adaptive execution engine (`adaptive_runner.py`) that auto-selects between execution tiers based on real-time GPU telemetry via nvidia-smi. Tier 2 runs sequential single-model with 30-second cooling pauses (our TaskFlow run: GPU temps 42°C → 52°C → 55°C, completed without crash). Tier 3 micro-mode halves context windows and drops to an 8B model with 60-second cooling when GPU exceeds 65°C. This isn't a limitation — it's production-grade infrastructure that solves a real deployment problem no other hackathon project has encountered, let alone solved.

We also engineered a pre-seeded context injection mechanism that guarantees evidence delivery even when small local models bypass the ReAct tool-calling loop — a documented failure mode we encountered and solved. The `fetch_context_for_product()` function retrieves RAG results at crew build time and injects them directly into task descriptions, structurally guaranteeing evidence grounding regardless of whether the LLM executes tool calls during inference. Every component — persona generation, evidence retrieval, adversarial debate, verdict synthesis, video frame analysis, screenshot matching, thermal management — is AI-native. Remove AI and there is no product.
